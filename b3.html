Panipat, Vishwas Patil’s debut literary work, revisits the third battle of Panipat (14 January 1761) between the Maratha forces and Ahmad Shah Abdali. The novel captures the courage of leaders like Jankoji Shinde, Nanasaheb Peshwa, and Sadashivrao Bhau, while exploring themes of valor, betrayal, despair, and unity. Originally written in Marathi (1988), the book has won 38 awards, sold over 200,000 copies, and has been translated into English and several Indian languages. A must-read for history and literature enthusiasts alike.
